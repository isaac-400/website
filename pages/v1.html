Some Other Poems - Volume 1

What is it called when your senses disagree?
When you see blue but feel red?
When you feel rhythm but hear no music?
When you hear rain hit the pavemen, steeping in the cracks
and it smells like tea?

What is it called when you touch the softness of their skin
but only feek the roughness of you own fingertips?
When you open your eyes wider in the dark, but the view gets blacker?
When you taste smoke in the air long before a fire starts?
When you hear a song but when you join in coughs break out?

Mulling in sensual confusion is human,
reconciling it is for poets,
and solving it, fools.
